Chapter 6 Core focus

In Chapter 6, the animals continue to build the Windmill, working like slaves. Despite their struggles, Napoleon decides to trade with humans, breaking one of the original resolutions.

The pigs move into the farmhouse and begin sleeping in beds. Squealer manipulates the commandments, rewriting the Fourth Commandment to state "No animal shall sleep in a bed with sheets." During a storm, the Windmill collapses, but Napoleon strategically blames it entirely on the exiled Snowball, offering a reward for his capture.

Key Historical Allegory: This represents the transition of the revolutionary class into a bureaucratic elite—mirroring how Soviet administrators and Communist Party officials hoarded luxuries and resources while directing the working class (the Stakhanovites).
